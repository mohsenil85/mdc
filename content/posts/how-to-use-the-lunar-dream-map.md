The Lunar Dream Map is a circular wheel for tracking three things at once: your dreams, your body, and the moon, across one lunar cycle. It grew out of my own morning practice, and it's the tool I hand to almost everyone I do dreamwork with. Here's how to use it.

## The three rings

The wheel has three rings, moving from the outside in:

- **The outer ring is for dreams.** Each morning, jot down a dream — even just a title, an image, a feeling. "The barn is on fire." "Swimming with my grandmother." You don't need the whole story.
- **The middle ring is for the date.**
- **The inner ring is for the body.** A word or two about how you woke: tender, buzzing, heavy, rested, wired. This is the somatic half of the practice, and it's the half most of us skip. Don't skip it.

Around the outer edge, you'll see the moon's phases — new, first quarter, full, last quarter — so you can see at a glance where the moon is as you chart. And in the very center, there's a space to **name the cycle** for yourself.

## The morning practice

Keep the map by your bed. When you wake, before you're pulled into the day — before breakfast, before the phone — take sixty seconds. Note the dream in the outer ring. Note your body in the inner ring. That's it. The noticing *is* the practice; you don't have to interpret anything.

If, like me, you're the parent of small children, this is an easy practice to land in the moment you wake, before you're thrown into serving up breakfast and brushing toddler teeth.

## Make it beautiful

I love to watercolor my Lunar Dream Map, or fill it in with colorful markers and metallic gel pens. I print mine on heavyweight watercolor paper, and I print one for my daughter too — she's three, so she's thrilled to have a matching watercolor project. Make it yours. Make it fun. These wheels become beautiful things to look back on.

## Why the moon?

Anchoring your dreams to the lunar cycle gives the practice a rhythm — a beginning and an end, a natural place to pause and reflect. If you menstruate, you can lay your cycle over the moon's and watch how your dreams shift across the month. And if you no longer bleed — in pregnancy, or after menopause — the moon becomes your anchor instead, a steady 29-day wheel to keep charting by.
